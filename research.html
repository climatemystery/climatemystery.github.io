Himalayan Monsoon

This study examines natural changes in the Eastern Himalayan monsoon over 43 years. Findings reveal wet/dry cycles, the influence of winds and SSTs, and minimal snowmelt impact on extreme rainfall. These insights are vital for flood prediction in Bhutan, Nepal, India, and China.

Elevation-dependent Warming

The first Eocene hothouse study confirmed elevation-dependent warming in the past. Mechanisms were revealed using energy budget analysis. Results hint at paradoxical elevation-dependent cooling under present-day boundaries. This research informs understanding of mountain climate futures.

Arabian Sea Monsoon

This research highlights changes in the summer monsoon low-level jet and ocean thermodynamics over the Arabian Sea. GFDL MOM5 model experiments confirmed momentum flux changes significantly boost upper ocean heat content, showing a tight ocean-monsoon link.
