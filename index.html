Himalayan Monsoon

This study examines the monsoon season in the Himalayas, which is of utmost importance to the people of South Asia. Every year, the heavy rainfall during the monsoon season causes floods that affect millions of people. This research aims to understand natural changes in the eastern Himalayan climate over the past 43 years, revealing interesting rainfall patterns: six dry years and seven wet years. Surprisingly, melting snow does not contribute significantly to extreme rainfall; instead, local winds and sea temperatures have major influence. This study shows that atmospheric processes shape the Himalayan monsoon, providing insights for better prediction of extreme weather and floods in Bhutan, Nepal, India, and China.
